Rule #1: Character Names in ALL CAPS

‚ùå

Wrong Way

‚úÖ

Right Way

SARAH: Can you hear me?

Instantly recognizable

üí° Why ALL CAPS?

Voice actors need to quickly identify who's speaking, especially during fast-paced recording sessions. ALL CAPS makes character names jump off the page!

Rule #2: Sound Effects (SFX)

üîä What are Sound Effects?

Everything your audience hears except dialogue and music - footsteps, doors opening, weather, explosions, ambient sounds.

üòï

Vague

SFX: Door
SFX: Car
üåü

Specific

SFX: Heavy wooden door creaks open slowly
SFX: Vintage car engine sputters to life

üéØ Pro Tip

Be specific! "Thunder crash" is better than "noise." "Footsteps on gravel" is better than "walking."

Rule #3: Direction Notes in (Brackets)

üé≠ What are Direction Notes?

Instructions for HOW lines should be delivered - emotional tone, volume, distance, accent, pace.

SARAH: (shouting over wind) Can you hear me?
JAKE: (whispered, scared) Something's out there.
NARRATOR: (mysterious, slow) Little did they know...

üòä Emotions

(happy), (angry), (confused), (excited)

üîä Volume

(shouting), (whispered), (mumbled)

üìç Location

(from distance), (off-mic), (echoing)

‚ö° Pace

(rushed), (slowly), (hesitant)
