Why Does Script Formatting Matter?

🎯 Clear Communication

Proper formatting ensures everyone on your production team knows exactly what to do - voice actors, sound engineers, and directors.

⚡ Professional Standards

Industry-standard formatting shows you're serious about your craft and makes your scripts easy to read and follow.

🧠 Better Final Product

Well-formatted scripts lead to smoother recordings, fewer mistakes, and more engaging audio experiences.

The Four Essential Elements

Every professional audio script uses these four building blocks:

📖 NARRATOR

Sets scenes, provides context, and guides the story forward.

🎭 CHARACTERS

The speakers in your story - always in ALL CAPITALS.

🔊 SFX

Sound Effects - all the background sounds and audio atmosphere.

🎵 MUSIC

Musical cues that set mood and guide emotions.

Rule #1: Character Names in ALL CAPS

❌

Wrong Way

sarah: Can you hear me?
Jake: I'm over here!

Hard to scan quickly

✅

Right Way

SARAH: Can you hear me?
JAKE: I'm over here!

Instantly recognizable

💡 Why ALL CAPS?

Voice actors need to quickly identify who's speaking, especially during fast-paced recording sessions. ALL CAPS makes character names jump off the page!

Rule #2: Sound Effects (SFX)

🔊 What are Sound Effects?

Everything your audience hears except dialogue and music - footsteps, doors opening, weather, explosions, ambient sounds.

😕

Vague

SFX: Door
SFX: Car
🌟

Specific

SFX: Heavy wooden door creaks open slowly
SFX: Vintage car engine sputters to life

🎯 Pro Tip

Be specific! "Thunder crash" is better than "noise." "Footsteps on gravel" is better than "walking."

Rule #3: Direction Notes in (Brackets)

🎭 What are Direction Notes?

Instructions for HOW lines should be delivered - emotional tone, volume, distance, accent, pace.

SARAH: (shouting over wind) Can you hear me?
JAKE: (whispered, scared) Something's out there.
NARRATOR: (mysterious, slow) Little did they know...

😊 Emotions

(happy), (angry), (confused), (excited)

🔊 Volume

(shouting), (whispered), (mumbled)

📍 Location

(from distance), (off-mic), (echoing)

⚡ Pace

(rushed), (slowly), (hesitant)

Rule #4: Music Cues

🎵 The Power of Music

Music guides your audience's emotions, sets the pace, and creates atmosphere. It's the invisible storyteller!

MUSIC: Tension builds with low, ominous tones
MUSIC: Upbeat jazz fades in
MUSIC: Romantic theme swells
MUSIC: Abrupt silence

🌅 Scene Setting

Establish time period, location, or mood right away

🔄 Transitions

Smooth cuts between scenes or time jumps

💓 Emotional Cues

Tell your audience how to feel about what's happening

⏰ Pacing

Speed up excitement or slow down for reflection
